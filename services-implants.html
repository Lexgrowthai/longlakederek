A Permanent Solution for Missing Teeth

Losing a tooth — whether through injury, decay, or gum disease — affects far more than your appearance. It changes the way you eat, the way you speak, and the way you feel about yourself. Over time, a missing tooth also causes the surrounding bone to deteriorate and neighbouring teeth to shift out of alignment, creating a cascade of oral health complications that become increasingly complex and costly to address. At Long Lake Family Dentistry, we offer dental implants as the most advanced, durable, and life-like solution available for replacing missing teeth.

Dental implants look, feel, and function just like your natural teeth — giving you back your smile and your confidence.

A dental implant is a small titanium post that is surgically placed into the jawbone, where it functions as an artificial tooth root. Titanium is uniquely biocompatible — over a period of several months, the implant fuses with the surrounding bone through a natural process called osseointegration. This creates an incredibly stable, permanent foundation onto which a custom-made porcelain crown is attached, producing a replacement tooth that looks, feels, and functions exactly like a natural tooth.

Unlike dentures or bridges, a dental implant is a standalone restoration. It does not rely on neighbouring teeth for support, does not require adhesives, and does not shift or slip when you eat or speak. Because the implant integrates directly with the jawbone, it also stimulates the bone in the same way a natural tooth root does — preventing the bone loss that inevitably follows tooth extraction. This preservation of bone structure helps maintain the natural contours of your face and jaw for decades to come.

Your Implant Journey — Step by Step

1

Comprehensive Consultation & Planning

We conduct a thorough oral examination, including 3D cone beam imaging, to assess your bone density, gum health, and bite alignment. This allows us to create a precise, personalised implant treatment plan tailored to you.

2

Implant Placement Surgery

Under local anaesthetic — with sedation options available for nervous patients — the titanium implant post is gently placed into the jawbone. The procedure is more comfortable than most patients expect, and most return to normal activities within a day or two.

3

Osseointegration & Healing

Over the following 3 to 6 months, the implant naturally fuses with the surrounding bone. During this period, you may wear a temporary restoration so you never have to go without a tooth. We monitor your progress closely at each check-in appointment.

4

Abutment Placement

Once osseointegration is complete, a small connector piece called an abutment is attached to the implant. This is a minor procedure that forms the anchor point for your final crown restoration.

5

Custom Crown Fitting

Your final porcelain crown is precision-crafted to match the exact shape, size, and colour of your surrounding natural teeth. Once fitted and adjusted for a perfect bite, your new tooth is complete — indistinguishable from the real thing.

With proper care, dental implants can last 20 years or more — often a lifetime. They require no special maintenance beyond your regular brushing, flossing, and routine dental visits. For most patients, the investment in a dental implant is the last tooth replacement they will ever need to make for that tooth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are dental implants painful? +
The implant surgery is performed under local anaesthetic, so you should feel pressure but no pain during the procedure. Most patients report that the discomfort afterward is milder than they expected — typically manageable with over-the-counter pain relief for 2–3 days. The healing process is gradual and generally comfortable.
How long do dental implants last? +
With proper oral hygiene and regular dental checkups, the implant fixture itself can last a lifetime. The crown placed on top typically lasts 10–15 years before it may need to be replaced due to normal wear. This makes implants the longest-lasting tooth replacement option available — and the most cost-effective over the long term.
Am I a candidate for dental implants? +
Most healthy adults with adequate jawbone density are good candidates for implants. Key requirements include healthy gums, sufficient bone to anchor the implant, and no uncontrolled systemic conditions that impair healing. Patients who have experienced bone loss may still qualify after a bone graft procedure. A consultation with X-rays will determine your eligibility.
How long does the implant process take from start to finish? +
The full implant process typically takes 3–6 months. After the implant is placed, a healing period of 3–4 months is needed for osseointegration — the process by which the implant fuses with the jawbone. Once fully healed, the abutment and final crown are placed. Cases requiring bone grafting may add additional time.
What is the success rate of dental implants? +
Dental implants have a long-term success rate of 95–98% in healthy patients. Factors that can affect success include smoking, uncontrolled diabetes, and poor oral hygiene. Choosing an experienced provider and following all aftercare instructions significantly improves outcomes. We discuss your individual risk factors thoroughly before proceeding.
Are dental implants covered by insurance? +
Coverage varies widely. Some insurance plans cover part of the cost — typically the crown portion — while others do not cover implants at all. We recommend checking your specific plan before your consultation. We can also discuss financing options to help make implants accessible regardless of your coverage situation.
How do I care for my dental implant? +
Implants are cared for just like natural teeth — brush twice daily, floss once a day, and attend regular checkups and cleanings. There are no special cleaning products required. Avoiding hard foods that could chip the crown and not smoking are the two most important things you can do to protect your implant long-term.
What happens if I don't replace a missing tooth? +
When a tooth is missing, the surrounding teeth gradually drift into the empty space, your bite changes, and the jawbone beneath begins to shrink (resorb) due to lack of stimulation. Over time this can cause a sunken facial appearance, difficulty chewing, and increased risk of losing neighbouring teeth. Replacing the tooth promptly — ideally with an implant — prevents all of these issues.
