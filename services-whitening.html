A Brighter Smile, Backed by Science

Your smile is often the first thing people notice, and it's one of the most powerful tools you have for making a lasting impression. Over time, however, even the most diligently cared-for teeth become stained and discoloured — the result of everyday foods and beverages like coffee, tea, red wine, and berries, as well as aging, certain medications, and lifestyle factors. At Long Lake Family Dentistry, our professional teeth whitening treatments offer a safe, clinically proven way to restore the natural brilliance of your smile in as little as one appointment.

Our professional whitening treatments deliver results far beyond what store-bought products can achieve.

Unlike over-the-counter whitening strips or toothpastes, professional whitening uses prescription-strength bleaching agents that penetrate deep into tooth enamel to break apart the molecular bonds of stain compounds. The result is a genuinely transformative improvement — not just surface-level brightening, but a thorough, even whitening that can lift your smile by six to eight shades in a single session. Our dentists carefully evaluate the condition of your teeth and gums before treatment to ensure you achieve optimal results while keeping sensitivity to an absolute minimum.

Our Whitening Options

We offer two flexible approaches to teeth whitening, designed to fit your schedule and lifestyle goals:

1

In-Office Power Whitening

Our most popular option for patients who want immediate, dramatic results. We apply a professional-strength bleaching gel directly to your teeth and use an activating light to accelerate the whitening process. In just one 60-minute appointment, you'll leave with a smile that's visibly and dramatically brighter.

2

Custom Take-Home Whitening Kits

For patients who prefer to whiten on their own schedule, we create custom-fitted whitening trays from impressions of your teeth. Paired with professional-grade whitening gel, these trays deliver far superior results compared to generic store-bought options — with the added benefit of a precise fit that minimizes gel contact with your gums.

3

Combination Approach

Many patients choose to begin with an in-office treatment for an immediate boost, then maintain and enhance their results over time using their custom take-home kit. This combination approach delivers the most striking long-term results.

What to Expect at Your Whitening Appointment

1

Consultation & Shade Assessment

We begin by evaluating your current tooth shade and discussing your whitening goals. We'll also check your gums and enamel to confirm you're a good candidate for whitening treatment.

2

Gum & Tooth Preparation

Your gums are carefully protected with a barrier to prevent gel contact. Your teeth are cleaned and dried to ensure even gel application.

3

Whitening Gel Application

Professional-strength whitening gel is applied evenly across all visible teeth. The gel is left to work for the prescribed time, with multiple application cycles as needed.

4

Results & Aftercare Guidance

We reveal your new shade against the before shade to demonstrate the difference. You'll receive personalized guidance on foods and drinks to avoid in the first 48 hours, plus tips for maintaining your brighter smile long-term.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is professional teeth whitening safe? +
Yes — when performed or supervised by a dental professional, teeth whitening is very safe. Our team evaluates your enamel and gum health before treatment to ensure the best outcome with minimal sensitivity. Over-the-counter products carry more risk because they're used without professional guidance.
Will whitening work on crowns, veneers, or fillings? +
Whitening gels only work on natural tooth enamel. Existing restorations such as crowns, veneers, and composite fillings will not change colour. If you have prominent restorations on visible teeth, we'll discuss the best approach — which may include replacing those restorations after whitening to achieve a uniform shade.
How long do whitening results last? +
Results typically last 12–24 months with proper maintenance. Avoiding staining foods and beverages, using a whitening toothpaste, and occasional touch-ups with your take-home kit will significantly extend the longevity of your results.
Will my teeth be sensitive after whitening? +
Some temporary sensitivity is normal and typically resolves within 24–48 hours. We use professional desensitizing agents to minimize this, and we can recommend a sensitivity-reducing toothpaste to use in the days surrounding your treatment.
How much does professional whitening cost? +
Whitening is considered cosmetic and is generally not covered by dental insurance. We offer competitive pricing for both in-office and take-home options. Contact our office for current pricing and to discuss which option best fits your goals and budget.
