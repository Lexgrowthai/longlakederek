Restore Your Smile and Your Confidence

Losing teeth — whether one, several, or all of them — affects far more than your appearance. It impacts how you eat, how you speak, your facial structure, and your self-confidence. At Long Lake Family Dentistry, we understand how personal and emotional tooth loss can be, and we approach denture care with the compassion and expertise that this important treatment deserves. Our custom-crafted dentures are designed to look natural, fit comfortably, and restore full function to your smile.

Our dentures are custom-crafted for each patient, ensuring a natural appearance and comfortable, secure fit.

Modern dentures bear little resemblance to the ill-fitting, obviously artificial appliances of the past. Today's dentures are fabricated from high-quality materials that closely mimic the natural translucency, colour variation, and texture of real teeth and gum tissue. We take detailed impressions and carefully assess your bite, facial proportions, and aesthetic preferences to create an appliance that fits well, functions effectively, and — most importantly — looks like your own natural teeth.

Types of Dentures We Provide

😁

Complete (Full) Dentures

For patients who have lost all of their teeth in the upper or lower arch (or both). Rests on the gum tissue and is held in place by suction and the muscles of the mouth. Can be enhanced with dental implants for a more secure fit.

🦷

Partial Dentures

For patients missing several teeth but retaining some natural teeth. A partial denture fills the gaps, prevents remaining teeth from shifting, and restores chewing function. Attaches to natural teeth with clasps or precision attachments.

⏱️

Immediate Dentures

Placed immediately after extractions so you're never without teeth. Serve as a temporary solution while your gums heal and your permanent dentures are fabricated. Require more frequent adjustments as your gum shape changes during healing.

🔩

Implant-Supported Dentures

Dentures anchored to dental implants for dramatically improved stability and retention. Eliminates slipping and discomfort, allows you to eat a wider range of foods, and helps preserve jaw bone. The most comfortable and functional denture option available.

The Denture Process — What to Expect

1

Consultation & Treatment Planning

We evaluate your oral health, discuss your goals, and recommend the most appropriate denture type. X-rays may be taken to assess the health of any remaining teeth and your jaw bone.

2

Impressions & Measurements

Precise impressions of your mouth are taken, along with measurements of your bite and jaw relationship. These are sent to our dental lab to begin fabrication.

3

Try-In Appointment

A wax model or trial denture is created for you to try. This allows us to evaluate the fit, appearance, and bite before the final denture is completed — ensuring you are happy with the look and feel before we finalize anything.

4

Delivery & Fitting

Your finished denture is delivered and carefully fitted. We make any necessary adjustments to ensure optimal comfort and function. You'll receive detailed care instructions to keep your denture in excellent condition.

5

Follow-Up & Ongoing Care

Follow-up appointments are important to monitor fit as your gum tissue naturally changes over time. Dentures typically require relining every few years to maintain a proper fit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to get dentures? +
The process typically takes 4–6 weeks from the initial consultation to receiving your final denture, including multiple appointments for impressions, try-ins, and fittings. Immediate dentures can be placed the same day as extractions, though they will require adjustments as healing progresses.
Will dentures look natural? +
Yes — modern dentures are designed to look very natural. We carefully select tooth shades, shapes, and sizes that complement your facial features and look appropriate for your age. Most people around you will not be able to tell you're wearing a denture unless you tell them.
Are dentures covered by dental insurance? +
Most dental insurance plans provide some coverage for dentures, typically 50% of the cost up to your annual maximum. Coverage varies significantly between plans, so we recommend confirming your benefits before treatment. Our team can assist you with the claims process.
How do I care for my dentures? +
Rinse your denture after eating, brush it daily with a soft brush and denture cleanser (not regular toothpaste), and soak it overnight in water or a denture solution. Always handle over a folded towel or filled sink as dentures can break if dropped. Continue brushing your gums and any remaining natural teeth.
Can I eat normally with dentures? +
Yes, though there is an adjustment period. Start with soft foods cut into small pieces, chewing on both sides simultaneously. Over time you'll be able to eat most foods comfortably. Some very hard or sticky foods (hard candies, very chewy foods) are best avoided with conventional dentures. Implant-supported dentures allow you to eat a much wider range of foods with greater confidence.
