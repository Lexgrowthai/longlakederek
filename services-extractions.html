Safe, Gentle, and Comfortable Extractions

At Long Lake Family Dentistry, our first priority is always to save your natural tooth. But when a tooth is too damaged, infected, or crowded to treat effectively, extraction is the most responsible option — and when done well, it relieves pain quickly and sets you up for a healthy recovery and replacement plan.

💡 We Always Explore Every Option First

We never recommend extraction lightly. Before removing any tooth, we thoroughly assess whether a filling, crown, or root canal could save it. Only when a tooth is truly unsalvageable — or when keeping it would put surrounding teeth and bone at risk — do we recommend extraction. We'll always explain your options clearly so you can make the best decision for your long-term oral health.

Our experienced team performs extractions with precision and care, minimizing discomfort and promoting fast healing.

Extractions are performed under local anaesthesia so you remain comfortable throughout. For patients with anxiety, we can discuss additional comfort options at your consultation. After the procedure, we provide thorough aftercare instructions and are available to answer any questions during your recovery.

Reasons a Tooth May Need to Be Removed

🦷 Severe decay that has destroyed too much tooth structure to restore
🦠 Advanced infection that has spread to the surrounding bone
🔴 Gum disease that has caused significant bone loss around the tooth
🦷 A broken or cracked tooth that cannot be repaired with a crown
😬 Overcrowding prior to orthodontic treatment
8️⃣ Impacted or problematic wisdom teeth

Types of Extractions

🪄

Simple Extraction

Performed on a tooth that is visible above the gum line. The tooth is loosened with a dental instrument and gently removed in one piece. Most extractions fall into this category and are completed quickly under local anaesthetic.

🔬

Surgical Extraction

Required when a tooth is broken below the gum line, has curved roots, or is impacted (like wisdom teeth). A small incision is made to access the tooth. Still performed under local anaesthetic and completed in-office in most cases.

8️⃣

Wisdom Tooth Removal

Wisdom teeth (third molars) frequently cause problems — impaction, crowding, infection, or partial eruption that traps bacteria. We assess wisdom teeth with panoramic X-rays and recommend removal when they pose a risk to your oral health.

🧒

Baby Tooth Extraction

Occasionally a primary (baby) tooth won't fall out on its own and needs to be removed to make way for the permanent tooth underneath. This is a straightforward procedure performed gently to keep children comfortable and at ease.

What to Expect During the Procedure

1

Examination & X-Rays

We take a digital X-ray of the tooth to assess its root shape, position, and the surrounding bone. This allows us to plan the extraction precisely and anticipate any complexity before we begin.

2

Local Anaesthesia

The area is thoroughly numbed with local anaesthetic. We wait until you confirm you're completely comfortable before proceeding. You may feel pressure or movement, but you should not feel pain.

3

Extraction

For a simple extraction, we gently loosen the tooth with a specialized instrument before lifting it free. Surgical extractions may involve a small incision to access the root. Most extractions take only a few minutes once you are numb.

4

Clot Formation & Gauze

Once the tooth is out, we place gauze over the socket and ask you to bite down gently to help a blood clot form. This clot is critical to healing — we'll explain exactly how to protect it at home.

5

Aftercare & Next Steps

We provide written aftercare instructions and discuss your tooth replacement options — such as a dental implant or bridge — if applicable. We schedule a follow-up and are available by phone if any concerns arise during healing.

Aftercare Tips for a Smooth Recovery

🩸

Bite on gauze for 30–45 min to help the clot form

🧊

Apply an ice pack (20 min on, 20 off) to reduce swelling

🍦

Eat soft foods for 24–48 hours; avoid hard or crunchy items

🚭

Avoid smoking — it significantly delays healing and risks dry socket

💊

Take pain relief as directed; most discomfort resolves in 2–3 days

🚫

Don't use a straw — suction can dislodge the blood clot

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a tooth extraction hurt? +
The procedure itself should not be painful — the area is thoroughly numbed with local anaesthetic before we begin. You may feel pressure or movement as the tooth is loosened, but you should not feel pain. After the anaesthetic wears off, some soreness is normal and is usually well-managed with over-the-counter pain relief like ibuprofen or acetaminophen.
How long does recovery take? +
Most patients feel back to normal within 2–3 days for a simple extraction. Surgical extractions and wisdom teeth removal may take 4–7 days. Full bone and gum healing takes several weeks, but this occurs without any noticeable discomfort after the first few days. Following the aftercare instructions we provide makes a significant difference in recovery speed.
What is dry socket and how do I avoid it? +
Dry socket occurs when the blood clot that forms in the extraction site is dislodged or dissolves before healing is complete, exposing the bone underneath. It causes intense pain usually starting 2–3 days after extraction. To avoid it: don't smoke, don't use a straw, avoid spitting forcefully, and follow our aftercare instructions closely. If you suspect dry socket, call us right away — it's easily treated.
Do I need to replace the extracted tooth? +
In most cases, yes — especially for permanent teeth. A missing tooth causes the surrounding teeth to drift over time, changes your bite, and leads to bone loss at the extraction site. We'll discuss your replacement options, which may include a dental implant, bridge, or partial denture depending on your situation and budget. We can begin planning your replacement at the same appointment.
Is tooth extraction covered by insurance? +
Simple extractions are typically covered as a basic dental service (usually 80–100% after deductible). Surgical extractions and wisdom tooth removal are usually covered as a major service (50–80%). Coverage depends on your specific plan. We recommend confirming your benefits before your appointment, and our team is happy to assist with that process.
