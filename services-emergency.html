Fast, Compassionate Emergency Dental Care

A dental emergency can happen without warning — a knocked-out tooth from a fall, an unbearable toothache that wakes you in the night, a cracked tooth from biting into something hard. Whatever the situation, Long Lake Family Dentistry is here to help. We reserve emergency appointment slots in our daily schedule specifically to ensure that patients in pain are seen as quickly as possible — often the same day you call.

Our team is equipped and ready to handle a full range of dental emergencies with speed and compassion.

Delaying care for a dental emergency can turn a manageable problem into a significantly more complex and costly one. A cracked tooth left untreated can develop into a fractured root. A tooth that could have been saved with prompt care may eventually require extraction. An abscess left unaddressed can spread to surrounding tissue and bone. Acting quickly — and calling our office first — is always the right move when something unexpected happens with your teeth or gums.

Common Dental Emergencies We Treat

🦷

Severe Toothache

Intense, persistent tooth pain can indicate decay that has reached the nerve, an abscess, or a cracked tooth. We diagnose the cause and provide immediate pain relief.

💥

Knocked-Out Tooth

Time is critical — a tooth reimplanted within 30–60 minutes has the best chance of survival. Keep the tooth moist and call us immediately.

⚡

Cracked or Fractured Tooth

A cracked tooth can cause sharp pain and sensitivity. Depending on the severity, treatment may involve a filling, crown, or other restorative care.

🔴

Dental Abscess

An abscess is a serious infection that can spread rapidly. Symptoms include swelling, fever, and throbbing pain. This requires urgent care — do not wait.

🩹

Lost Filling or Crown

A lost restoration leaves your tooth vulnerable to sensitivity and further damage. We can replace or recement your filling or crown quickly to protect the tooth.

🩸

Gum or Soft Tissue Injury

Lacerations to the lips, gums, cheeks, or tongue from trauma should be evaluated. We can treat injuries and advise when referral to a specialist is needed.

What to Do While You're on Your Way

1

Call Us First

Phone 705-523-1004 immediately. Describing your situation allows our team to prepare for your arrival and provide first-aid guidance over the phone.

2

For a Knocked-Out Tooth

Handle the tooth by the crown (not the root). If dirty, rinse gently with milk or saline — not tap water. Keep it moist by placing it in milk, saline, or between your cheek and gum. Get to us within 30 minutes for the best chance of reimplantation.

3

For Severe Pain or Swelling

Take over-the-counter pain relief such as ibuprofen as directed on the package. Apply a cold compress (10 minutes on, 10 minutes off) to reduce swelling. Do not apply heat to a dental swelling.

4

For Bleeding

Apply gentle, firm pressure with clean gauze or a cloth. If bleeding does not slow after 20 minutes of continuous pressure, proceed to the nearest emergency room.

Frequently Asked Questions

What counts as a dental emergency? +
Any situation causing severe pain, significant swelling, uncontrolled bleeding, a knocked-out tooth, or an injury that risks permanent damage qualifies as an emergency. When in doubt, call us — we'll help you determine the urgency and whether you need to come in right away.
Do you accept patients who aren't currently registered at your clinic? +
Absolutely. We welcome new patients in emergency situations. Call us, explain your situation, and we'll do everything we can to see you promptly. You don't have to be an existing patient to receive emergency care.
What if my emergency happens after hours? +
Please call our office line — we have an after-hours message with guidance. For life-threatening emergencies or severe facial swelling affecting your airway, always call 911 or go to the nearest hospital emergency department immediately.
Can a knocked-out tooth really be saved? +
Yes — if you act quickly. A tooth reimplanted within 30 minutes has an excellent chance of survival. Keep the tooth moist (in milk, saline, or in your cheek) and get to us as fast as possible. The sooner you arrive, the better the prognosis.
Is emergency dental care covered by insurance? +
Most dental insurance plans cover emergency examinations and any necessary treatments at your standard coverage rate. Bring your insurance information to your appointment and our administrative team will submit the claim on your behalf.
