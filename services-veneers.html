The Gold Standard in Smile Transformation

Porcelain veneers are widely regarded as one of the most powerful cosmetic dental treatments available — and for good reason. These custom-crafted, wafer-thin shells of dental porcelain are bonded to the front surfaces of your teeth, instantly correcting a wide range of cosmetic concerns and creating a beautifully uniform, natural-looking smile. At Long Lake Family Dentistry, we design every set of veneers to complement your unique facial features, skin tone, and smile aesthetics, ensuring results that look genuinely natural rather than artificial.

Each veneer is individually crafted and colour-matched to create a seamless, natural-looking transformation.

One of the most remarkable qualities of porcelain as a veneer material is how closely it mimics the optical properties of natural tooth enamel. It reflects light in a similar way, creating that luminous quality that characterizes a truly beautiful smile. Porcelain is also highly stain-resistant, meaning your veneers will maintain their brightness for years with proper care — unlike natural enamel, which continues to absorb staining compounds over time.

Veneers are a versatile solution that can address multiple cosmetic concerns simultaneously, making them particularly valuable for patients who have several issues they'd like to correct at once. Rather than pursuing multiple separate treatments, a single set of veneers can reshape, brighten, and unify your smile in just two or three appointments.

What Veneers Can Correct

💛 Deeply stained or discoloured teeth that don't respond to whitening
💥 Chipped or slightly fractured front teeth
📐 Mildly misaligned or uneven teeth
↔️ Small gaps between teeth (diastema)
📏 Teeth that are too short, small, or irregularly shaped
🪨 Worn enamel giving a dull or aged appearance

The Veneer Process — Step by Step

1

Smile Design Consultation

We discuss your smile goals, show you examples of veneer results, and assess whether veneers are the right solution for your specific concerns. Digital imaging may be used to preview potential outcomes.

2

Tooth Preparation

A very small amount of enamel (typically 0.3–0.7mm) is gently removed from the front surface of each tooth to make room for the veneer. Local anesthesia ensures this is completely comfortable. Impressions are then taken and sent to our dental lab.

3

Temporary Veneers

While your custom veneers are being fabricated (typically 2 weeks), we place temporary veneers so you can preview your new smile and leave our office looking great.

4

Bonding Your Permanent Veneers

When your porcelain veneers arrive from the lab, we carefully bond each one to your teeth using a strong dental adhesive. We verify the shade, shape, and fit before permanently cementing them in place.

5

Final Check & Bite Adjustment

We check your bite and make any fine adjustments to ensure perfect comfort. A follow-up appointment is scheduled to review your results and ensure your complete satisfaction.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are veneers permanent? Can they be removed? +
Because a small amount of enamel is removed during preparation, traditional porcelain veneers are considered a permanent, irreversible treatment. This is why the consultation and planning stages are so important — we take the time to ensure you are fully informed and confident in your decision before proceeding.
Do veneers look fake or obvious? +
Not with modern porcelain veneers designed by an experienced dental team. We customize the shade, translucency, shape, and size of each veneer to complement your natural features. The goal is a smile that looks genuinely beautiful and natural — not artificial or "Hollywood white" unless that is specifically what you want.
How long do porcelain veneers last? +
With proper care, porcelain veneers typically last 10–15 years or longer. Avoiding biting hard objects (ice, pens, fingernails), wearing a night guard if you grind your teeth, and maintaining excellent oral hygiene will all extend the life of your veneers significantly.
Are veneers covered by dental insurance? +
Veneers are generally considered a cosmetic procedure and are not covered by most dental insurance plans. However, if veneers are being used to repair fractured or structurally compromised teeth, partial coverage may apply. We recommend checking with your insurer and we are happy to help you explore your options.
Is the veneer procedure painful? +
Local anesthesia is used during the preparation appointment, so the procedure itself is comfortable. Some patients experience mild sensitivity in the days following preparation while wearing temporary veneers — this resolves once the permanent veneers are placed.
