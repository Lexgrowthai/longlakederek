Restore Your Teeth Naturally

Cavities are among the most common dental problems affecting Canadians of all ages — and the good news is that modern composite resin fillings make treating them faster, more comfortable, and more aesthetically pleasing than ever before. At Long Lake Family Dentistry, we use only tooth-coloured composite fillings because we believe your dental restorations should be virtually invisible and seamlessly blend with your natural smile. Gone are the days of conspicuous silver amalgam fillings; today's composite materials are not only beautiful but exceptionally durable.

Our composite fillings are colour-matched to your natural tooth shade for a completely seamless result.

Composite resin is a versatile dental material that bonds directly to the structure of your tooth, meaning we can preserve more of your natural tooth than traditional amalgam restorations require. This tooth-conservative approach is better for the long-term health of your tooth. Beyond cavities, composite resin is also an excellent material for repairing chipped, cracked, or worn teeth — a process often called dental bonding — making it one of the most useful tools in modern cosmetic and restorative dentistry.

Our team will perform a thorough examination and take digital X-rays to assess the extent of any decay before recommending treatment. In many cases, catching a cavity early means a smaller, simpler filling and a more comfortable appointment. This is one of the many reasons we emphasize regular check-up visits — small problems treated promptly rarely become large, complex ones.

What to Expect at Your Filling Appointment

1

Examination & X-Rays

We examine the affected tooth and take digital X-rays to determine the exact size and depth of the cavity, ensuring we have a full picture before treatment begins.

2

Local Anaesthesia

A local anesthetic is applied to numb the area completely. We take our time with this step to ensure you feel nothing during the procedure — your comfort is our first priority.

3

Decay Removal

Using precise dental instruments, we carefully remove all decayed tissue while preserving as much healthy tooth structure as possible.

4

Composite Placement & Bonding

The tooth-coloured composite resin is applied in layers, each hardened with a curing light. We shape and contour the material to match your natural tooth perfectly.

5

Bite Check & Polish

We check your bite to ensure the filling sits perfectly, then polish the restoration so it feels completely smooth and natural in your mouth.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I need a filling? +
Common signs include tooth sensitivity to hot, cold, or sweet foods, a visible dark spot or hole in the tooth, or pain when biting. However, many cavities have no symptoms at all, which is why regular check-ups with X-rays are so important for catching decay early.
Does getting a filling hurt? +
With modern local anaesthesia, the procedure itself is generally painless. You may feel pressure or vibration but not pain. After the freezing wears off (typically 2–4 hours later), some mild soreness is normal and resolves within a day or two.
How long do composite fillings last? +
With proper care, composite fillings typically last 10–15 years or longer. Avoiding extremely hard foods, maintaining good oral hygiene, and attending regular check-ups all contribute to the longevity of your restoration.
Can old silver amalgam fillings be replaced with composite? +
Yes — many patients choose to have their old amalgam fillings replaced with tooth-coloured composites for cosmetic and health reasons. We evaluate each filling individually to determine whether replacement is appropriate and discuss the best timing.
Are fillings covered by dental insurance? +
Most dental insurance plans cover composite fillings for back teeth at the amalgam rate, with the patient covering the difference for the composite upgrade — though many plans now fully cover composites. We recommend contacting your insurer beforehand, and our team is happy to help you understand your benefits.
